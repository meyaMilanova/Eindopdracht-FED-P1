Sun

There is a gap,
Like an empty room,
A rubber room with rats,
The rats made me crazy, --crazy,
I was crazy once, they locked me in a room, a-, scratch that.
Now for real. A gap.
It grew daily,
Small pieces falling down, each bigger than the last.
From every lacked effort to the glasses you never put off to see me.
Even after all my sad attempts you still didn't look.
You saw it, you saw me,
But you didn't look,
Because I was simply not your sun.
Probably because I didnt feel like one then.

When you were here, sadness was my best friend.
But now that you're gone, sadness is a stranger.
And now I see the sunrays.
I feel happiness and I'm grasping on to it like oxygen.
Mainly because I don't want to go back,
Back to the time pain was home.
The home you made and left me in alone.
It didn't have much inside,
Just once said and immediately after forgotten pretty words.

Finally, after all this time, that lock on that door that kept me 'home',
That blockage, that one sided pointless promise, that delusionâ€¦
Broke, I broke it.
And rather than breaking me as well, it liberated me.
Showed me happiness,
Made me find love for myself,
Made me realize that even though it feels the worst,
it's no hell.
Just a chance to change.
Spoiler, I'm grasping on to it too.
And I'll continue to,
Till the moment I become a sun,
A sun but to another.
Another with no glasses for cover,
Another, that doesn't act but really is a lover.

-MTM
